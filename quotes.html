Master's quotes

Frank Underwood's quotes are a testament to his unparalleled majesty in the world of power and politics. Each word, a carefully crafted symphony, echoes with authority and cunning wisdom, leaving an indelible mark on the landscape of political intrigue. Underwood's quotes are not merely words; they are regal commands that define the essence of ruthlessness and mastery in the pursuit of ambition.

Here are the top quotes:

#10

"There are two kinds of pain. The sort of pain that makes you strong, or useless pain. The sort of pain that’s only suffering. I have no patience for useless things."

#09

"There’s no better way to overpower a trickle of doubt than with a flood of naked truth."

#08

"A great man once said, everything is about sex. Except sex. Sex is about power."

#07

"I’ve always loathed the necessity of sleep. Like death, it puts even the most powerful men on their backs."

#06

"The nature of promises, Linda, is that they remain immune to changing circumstances."

#05

" Every kitten grows up to be a cat. They seem so harmless at first. Small, quiet, lapping up their saucer of milk. But once their claws get long enough, they draw blood. Sometimes from the hand that feeds them. For those of us climbing to the top of the food chain, there can be no mercy. There is but one rule: hunt or be hunted"

#04

"Such a waste of talent. He chose money over power. In this town, a mistake nearly everyone makes. Money is the MC-mansion in Sarasota that starts falling apart after 10 years. Power is the old stone building that stands for centuries. I cannot respect someone who doesn’t see the difference."

#03

" Love of family. Most politicians are permanently chained to that slogan. Family values. But when you cozy up to hookers and I find out, I will make that hypocrisy hurt."

#02

"I know you take a lot of pride in your manhood, Bob, it's impressive. But as big as it is, Linda can still shut the door on it."

#01

"That's right. We don't submit to terror. We make the terror."
